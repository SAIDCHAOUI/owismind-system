Level 1 - The Experience

The Experience

A guided tour of OWIsMind: screen layout, asking questions, reading verified evidence, managing your budget and browsing the agent library.

Getting started

OWIsMind is a chat portal that lets you query your business data in natural language, without writing a single line of code. You ask a question the way you would ask a knowledgeable colleague and the application returns a quantified answer grounded in a real SQL result - along with every detail of how that result was produced.

The application lives inside your Dataiku DSS instance as a plugin webapp. There is nothing to install on your workstation: open the address your administrator gave you in your usual browser. You are already authenticated by DSS; OWIsMind reuses that session, so there is no new password or login form.

In Flux

If you see a "Storage not configured" message in the center of the screen when you open the application, it means an administrator has not yet chosen the SQL storage connection in the webapp settings. This is not an error on your side: notify your administrator. As long as this setting is missing, the chat remains unavailable.

Your identity, resolved automatically

OWIsMind recognizes who you are without asking you anything. On opening, the application retrieves your identity from DSS and derives a display name from your login identifier - for example, said.chaoui becomes "Said". Your initials appear in a chip at the bottom left of the screen.

What this means for you

You only see your conversations. No one else can read your exchanges, and you do not see those of other users.

You only see the agents authorized by the administrator. The list of available agents is set on the server side, not in the browser.

Administrator vs. standard user

The very first user who opens the application after it is configured becomes an administrator and gains access to an Administration console. All following users are standard users. Administrators enable agents, author their profiles, and manage user budgets.

The workflow is immediate. The home screen shows a blank input area in the center - there is no intermediate page to navigate through.

"Break down revenues by solution line."

"Top 10 customers by turnover."

Only one domain is genuinely covered by an agent today: revenues (the DRIVE_Revenues dataset). If you ask about another domain (tickets, satisfaction, etc.), the orchestrator honestly answers that it does not yet have an agent for that domain - and never claims the data does not exist.

The title shown in the list is derived automatically from your first message (no entry required). To start over from scratch, click New conversation; your previous conversation remains in the sidebar, intact.

The center column is where the conversation happens. You type in the input bar at the bottom, watch the live timeline while the agent works, and read the response in the thread above. This section covers all the controls of that center column.

Writing a good question

OWIsMind is not a guessing engine: the more precise your request, the better the response. The input bar carries an explicit placeholder: "Describe your request as precisely as possible".

Choosing an agent

The agent selector (to the left of the input bar) shows the agent that will answer your next question. By default it is the OWIsMind_orchestrator - the generalist entry point that understands your question, routes it to the right specialist, and writes the analysis. The list always comes from the server: you only see agents that an administrator has enabled for your account.

Honesty firewall

If you ask about a domain not yet equipped (for example tickets), the orchestrator answers honestly that it does not yet have an agent for that domain. It will never say the data "does not exist" - this is a deliberate product rule called the honesty firewall.

Choosing a response mode: Eco, Medium, High

The mode selector (next to the agent picker) is a cost/quality slider. A small pill displays the current mode with a colored dot (green for Eco, orange for Medium, red for High). Clicking it opens an explanation window with the three modes side by side, with cost and speed gauges.

Eco (default)

1/5

Recommended. Fast, economical. Best for the vast majority of everyday questions.

Medium

3/5

Moderate cost. Faster. For analyses that require a bit more finesse.

High

5/5

High cost, more deliberate. For complex questions that warrant in-depth reasoning.

SQL accuracy is the same in all modes

The analytical SQL is always written by the same engine (the Semantic Model Query tool, on a fixed Sonnet model). Switching mode does not change the accuracy of the figure - it mainly changes the quality of the wording and the cost. Stay in Eco for most questions; reserve High for those that truly warrant in-depth reasoning.

Your choice takes effect only after you click Apply in the mode window. Cancel closes it without changing anything. The mode window also shows a reminder that the monthly budget of $50 is shared across all modes - the more powerful the mode, the faster the budget is consumed.

A cost tracking line appears under each response: input tokens, output tokens and the estimated cost of that exchange.

The live execution timeline

When you send a question, the agent works before it answers: it understands your request, grounds your terms onto exact database values, runs a SQL query, and drafts the response. OWIsMind makes this visible as a live timeline of steps in the agent's response bubble.

During the generation you see steps as they happen (planning, routing to the revenue specialist, executing the query, writing). Each step shows a human-readable label and a timer. To avoid overwhelming you, only the most recent five steps are displayed at once.

Once the response is finished, all steps collapse into a single collapsible header ("Agent steps - N steps - total time"). You can expand it to review what happened, but the written response is always the main element.

In Flux: step labels

The exact list of steps displayed depends on the labels emitted by the agents, which are evolving. An unknown label is simply humanized, so a new kind of step never breaks the display.

Stopping a generation

While an agent is working, the send button becomes a stop button (square "stop"). Clicking it requests a cooperative stop - the backend cuts off cleanly between two pieces of the response rather than killing the work abruptly.

You will first see a blinking "Stopping..." indicator with a spinner. Then the final state appears:

  • If the agent had already written some text, the partial is kept and marked "Generation stopped".
  • If it had not yet produced anything, OWIsMind displays "Response interrupted" rather than an empty bubble.

The stopped response stays in the thread - it is not removed.

Conversation branches: editing and regenerating

An OWIsMind conversation is not a simple linear list - it is a tree. You can start again from any previous question without losing anything.

Edit your question

Hover over your bubble and click the edit icon. An inline text area opens; on validating, OWIsMind creates a new sibling branch with your reformulated question. The old version is never erased. Use this to correct or refine a poorly worded request.

Regenerate the response

Under an agent response, the "Regenerate" button reruns the same prompt to obtain another response as a new sibling branch. Useful if the first response does not suit you, or if you want to replay in a different mode.

When a point in the conversation has several versions, navigation arrows appear with a counter such as "2/3". The left and right arrows move between versions.

Feedback

Under each agent response, a thumbs-up and a thumbs-down button let you rate it. A thumbs-down opens a small window to specify what went wrong (wrong response, incomplete, off topic, or a free comment). A thumbs-up can also be commented on via the "..." menu. Your feedback is stored and helps the OWI team improve the agents.

Understanding the results

When you ask a question, OWIsMind does not simply hand you a figure: it opens an evidence panel called Evidence Studio to the right of the chat. This panel answers one question: "Can I trust this result, and how was it obtained?"

The key point to remember is this: every figure shown comes from a real SQL query run against the data, never from an estimate produced by the language model. The panel rebuilds the explanation from that real query, automatically and deterministically - the same result always yields the same explanation. No language model is involved at evidence time.

Live re-read

Evidence Studio re-reads the data right now, at the moment you look at it. Only the "Result used by the agent" block shows what the agent actually saw when it answered. If the data has changed since the response, the exploration rows here may differ from those the agent used.

The panel tabs

Tab What you see there Always shown?
Evidence The heart of the panel: badge, sources, filter chips, calculation steps, captured result, exploration table, SQL. Always present. Yes
Chart An interactive chart (line, bars or pie) built server-side from the captured result. Appears only if the agent requested a visual display. No
Table The exact result received by the agent, presented as a table. No
KPI A key figure highlighted, with an optional variation (green arrow if up, red if down). No

Switching tabs does not affect the conversation thread. Charts, tables and KPIs are built server-side: the agent only states which field goes on the x-axis or y-axis, and the server does the rest. If the data does not lend itself to a plot, the tab shows an honest message rather than a false chart.

The trust badge: the five levels

At the very top of the Evidence tab, a pill summarizes the verification level. The color rule is deliberate and never includes green - green would give a false impression of total guarantee.

Filter chips (editable)

The filters section breaks down the query's WHERE condition into chips - one per filter, in column/value language. Each chip is editable (you can change its values via a distinct-values selector) and removable (removing a chip broadens the scope). An "Add a filter" button lets you add filters on any column; an "Agent version" button reverts to the original filters.

Changing a filter drives the exploration table below. It never rewrites the agent's response: you are exploring the source data freely, not changing the answer. A dashed orange border on the filter area signals that you have diverged from the agent's original filters.

Security: no raw SQL ever sent from the browser

When you change a filter, the application never sends SQL to the server. It only transmits the column, the operator and the chosen values. The server itself rebuilds the query, bounded and read-only. You never choose the table or the connection.

Captured result and drill

The "Result used by the agent" section shows the exact rows the agent received to formulate its response, in a small bounded table along with the total row count. Two cases:

Captured result

The table shows the first rows actually used. If the result was truncated, a note "Result truncated - first rows only." makes it clear.

Result not captured

An honest line indicates "The exact result used by the agent was not kept for this response." Capture is best-effort and not guaranteed. The panel remains useful for everything else (badge, sources, calculation, exploration, SQL).

When the backend has certified the drill as reliable, a chevron appears at the end of each result row. Clicking it "dives" into the source rows that make up that result. The drill is offered only when proven reliable: if a column cannot be tied back with certainty, the chevron is deliberately hidden.

Exploration table and collapsed SQL

Below the drill banner, the "Explore the source data" section shows the rows of the source table, loaded lazily as you scroll (up to 500 rows). This table reflects the current chips. It re-reads today's data, which may differ from what the agent saw.

At the very bottom, a collapsed "Technical details (SQL)" panel shows the exact query run by the agent, formatted and colored. A copy button copies it as-is. This is the ultimate level of transparency: the evidence hides nothing.

Degraded mode

When the interactive view is not available (for example the queried table does not match any SQL dataset in the project), the panel shows the "Stated by the agent" badge and an explanatory message, and still offers the raw query when it exists. Nothing is invented: you are told honestly what is missing.

In Flux: result capture and KPI tab

Capture of the exact result (and the Chart / Table / KPI tabs that derive from it) is best-effort and may be absent on the instance. The KPI tab is wired on the interface side, but its appearance depends on the agent requesting that type of display. These behaviors are detailed in the backend documentation.

When the budget is exhausted

When your spend reaches your limit and enforcement is on, the budget card shows a red alert flag. Simultaneously, in the chat:

The block is enforced server-side at /chat/start (HTTP 402). If the budget status cannot be read due to a backend error, the request is allowed through rather than blocking you incorrectly. The spend is still recorded.

Usage history

Below the gauge, a mini-grid shows your current-month request count and token total. Further down, a four-tile grid shows lifetime statistics: tokens this month (with input/output sub-lines), spend this month, lifetime spend, and last activity date.

Context window preference

At the bottom of the page, a preference card lets you set how many recent messages OWIsMind sends to the agent as context for each new exchange. Default: 20 messages; minimum 10; maximum 50; step 10. Lowering this value makes each exchange cheaper and faster. Raising it lets the agent refer to earlier parts of a long conversation.

Agents library and administration

OWIsMind exposes a growing set of AI agents. This section covers two connected features: the Agents library, available to every user for browsing and reading agent profiles; and the Administration console, visible only to administrators, for enabling agents, authoring profiles, managing users and controlling monthly budgets.

The Agents library (all users)

Click Agents in the sidebar to open the agent library. It shows all agents currently enabled for your account. The list always comes from the server - if an agent does not appear here, it is not available on your instance.

The library opens on a grid of cards. Above the grid, a search bar filters by name, tagline or description; a count line ("N agent(s)") updates live. Each card shows:

  • A square icon tile (orange glyph on a white square with a 1px border).
  • An optional badge (Default, New, Beta) in the top-right corner.
  • The agent name (bold, 16px) and a tagline (short punchline in orange text, AA-safe).
  • A description (up to 3 lines, clipped with "..." if longer).
  • A footer line with the number of documented tools, or "View profile" if none.

Zero hardcoded content

Every profile (tagline, description, capabilities, tools, icon, badge) is authored by an administrator. If a profile has not been filled in yet, the card reads: "This agent's profile has not been filled in by an administrator yet." This is an honest state, never invented content.

Detail view

Clicking a card navigates to the agent's detail page and shows the full editorial profile: a hero header with a large icon tile (56x56), the agent name and tagline; a description paragraph (up to 700 characters); a two-column grid with capabilities (up to 8 bullet items) and exposed tools (chip-style labels in monospace, up to 16 items); and a "Start a conversation" button that pre-selects this agent in the chat input and navigates to the Chat page.

The Administration console (admins only)

The first user to open OWIsMind after the webapp is configured becomes the administrator. All subsequent users are standard users. The Administration console is server-gated: the backend checks whether the authenticated user has the admin flag before serving any admin route.

Honesty contract

The profile is the only source of agent card content. There is no fallback copy hardcoded anywhere in the frontend. An agent whose profile has not been authored shows an honest "profile to complete" state. The backend's validate_agent_meta() sanitizes every field on write (icon must be in the server-side whitelist; badge must be one of default / new / beta or empty). The profile is returned via GET /agents without leaking the Dataiku agent_id or the source project.

Managing users

The Users tab lists every user who has ever opened the webapp. For each user the table shows: display name, groups, and an admin toggle (Make admin / Revoke admin). You cannot revoke admin from yourself if you are the last administrator. You cannot promote a user who has never opened the webapp.

Quotas and budgets

The Quotas & budgets tab manages the monthly per-user budget system. It has three panels:

Global settings

Set the default monthly limit (in dollars) and toggle whether the limit is enforced (blocking requests) or just tracked. Default limit: $50. Click "Save configuration" to apply.

Temporary global boost

Raise every user's limit for a limited time - useful during a busy reporting period. Set a temporary limit amount and a duration in days. The boost reverts automatically when it expires. Click "Apply boost" to activate; "Remove the global boost" to cancel early.

Per-user limits table

Lists all users with their current-month spend, effective limit, remaining amount and source (Default / Global boost / Custom / Temporary). Tick one or more users, fill in a new limit amount and duration (Permanent or Temporary: 7, 30 or 90 days), then click "Apply limit". Click "Reset to default" to remove personal overrides.

FAQ and troubleshooting

Practical answers to everyday questions and the right course of action for common situations.

Frequently asked questions

Which agents can I query today?

By default, your questions are routed to the OWIsMind_orchestrator, which routes your request to the appropriate specialist. In v3, a single domain is genuinely equipped: revenue (the SalesDrive_revenue_expert sub-agent). It knows every revenue figure in the DRIVE_Revenues dataset, across all Phases: ACTUALS (default), BUDGET, FORECAST, Q3F, HLF.

Why trust an answer?

Every numeric answer comes with the Evidence Studio panel that re-derives, without any AI, how the figure was produced. The badge is never green: solid orange = certified, dashed orange = partial, gray = declared. See Understanding the results above.

Is there a monthly budget?

Yes. Every user gets a rolling monthly credit in US dollars (default $50) that resets automatically on the 1st of each month. Your current spend, limit, remaining amount and reset date are visible on the My account page. When your monthly budget is exhausted, the input bar is blocked and a banner appears. If you need a higher limit, contact your administrator.

Are my conversations private?

You only see your conversations and the agents authorized for you. Identity is resolved on the server side from your browser session, never from the content you send.

Common problems

The agent says it does not have an agent for this domain

This is intended behavior, not a failure. The orchestrator applies a honesty firewall: it never invents a figure and never claims data "does not exist". The only form of "no" it allows is: "I do not yet have an agent for this domain" (a capability gap). If you genuinely need that domain covered, report it to your administrator - adding an agent is a configuration action on the agent side.

Report this to support

If the orchestrator claims that a figure "does not exist" or "is zero" without having consulted the specialist, that is abnormal - it violates the honesty firewall. Report it to support.

The answer takes a while or drops in one block at the end

This is normal and tied to the architecture: SSE streaming is buffered by DSS, so a polling transport is used. What is truly "live" is the timeline. A request may also take time because it chains several steps (understanding, grounding terms to exact values, running SQL, formatting). Follow the timeline rather than the text area.

"Stopping..." stays displayed for a moment after I stop

The stop is cooperative: the backend can only cut between two chunks. If a model call or a SQL query is in progress, this may take a few seconds. The "Stopping..." indicator and spinner confirm the stop was received. You have nothing else to do but wait.

The answer is empty or no figure is displayed

Three cases to distinguish:

  • No row matches your scope. The specialist searched but the filter returns nothing. Check the scope line in the response and try broadening or correcting a term. Remember: you never sum across Phases; specify the intended Phase.
  • The typed term does not match any exact value. A typo or an approximate label may find nothing. Use the exact name of the customer, product or zone.
  • The interactive Evidence view is unavailable. The panel shows an explicit message and still shows you the exact query executed. The chat answer remains valid.

The send is blocked with a budget banner

Your monthly budget has been reached. Wait for the automatic reset on the 1st of the month, or contact your administrator to raise your limit temporarily or permanently.

When to contact an administrator

Call an administrator (rather than simply reloading the page) in these cases:

  • "storage not configured": the SQL connection is not chosen. Only an admin configures it.
  • An agent you need does not appear in the selector: enabling it is an admin setting.
  • An agent's card shows "profile to complete": the agent is enabled but its profile has not been authored yet.
  • Your monthly budget is exhausted before the end of the month: an admin can raise your limit.
  • Generalized slowness in all modes, or repeated busy errors: may signal a backend to restart or a loaded instance.
  • The orchestrator claims data "does not exist" instead of consulting the specialist: report as a deviation from the honesty firewall.
