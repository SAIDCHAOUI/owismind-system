# Account Manager Perspective: OWIsMind - When It Changes My Day

---

## My 6 Moments of Value

**1. The live meeting ambush**

Client CFO asks mid-call: "Your roaming revenues dropped 18% last quarter - what happened?" Today I say "let me come back to you on that." With OWIsMind I ask in plain French, get a breakdown by product line and geography in 15 seconds, confirm it was one specific solution line that underperformed in two markets, and I have a bar chart I can screenshare immediately. I keep control of the room. I close that meeting instead of spending 3 days recovering credibility.

OUTPUT needed: a chart I can screenshare live OR paste into the deck I already have open, plus the exact numbers in a one-liner I can read out loud without misquoting.

**2. Monday morning prep - 20 accounts in 90 minutes**

Every Monday I prepare for the week: 20 accounts, I need to know which ones are down, which are up, who has a renewal coming, who exceeded budget. Today that takes half a day pulling from three systems. With OWIsMind I ask "show me my portfolio actuals vs budget for this month, flag anything below 80%" and I get a ranked table in seconds. I annotate it and I walk into every call prepared.

OUTPUT needed: a clean ranked table (the kind I can paste into Excel or screenshot for my manager). A PDF briefing sheet per account if I have time to review one the night before.

**3. Pre-call account 360 in 3 minutes**

I have a call with Airbus Maroc in 30 minutes. I ask: "Airbus Maroc - YTD actuals vs budget, which products, vs same period last year, and what was their peak month." I get the full picture, properly formatted in euros, with the breakdown. I walk into that call knowing more than the client expects me to know. That is how I build trust with C-levels.

OUTPUT needed: a formatted one-pager I can pull up on my phone. Ideally one I can send to my manager 2 minutes before as a pre-call brief.

**4. Answering a client email at 11pm**

Client sends a revenue question at 8pm. Today I either ignore it until tomorrow (bad) or dig into systems for 45 minutes (also bad). With OWIsMind on my phone or laptop I ask the question, verify the number against what I know is true, copy the answer into an email reply. 4 minutes instead of 45. I look responsive. I sleep.

OUTPUT needed: a draft reply email with the number embedded, subject line, professional tone. I review, adjust one sentence, send. I never send anything the system generated without reading it - but I need the first draft.

**5. Quarterly business review slide in 20 minutes instead of 3 hours**

QBR preparation is a grind. Building the revenue evolution slide, the product mix pie, the YoY comparison. With OWIsMind I ask for actuals Q1-Q4 by product, get the data, get the chart, export a PNG of that chart directly into my PowerPoint. The 3-hour prep becomes 20 minutes. I spend the saved time on the narrative and the ask.

OUTPUT needed: a chart PNG I can drop straight into PowerPoint. A formatted data table I can paste. Not a full deck (I customize too much) - just the pre-built blocks.

**6. Spotting an opportunity before the client does**

Account is running 92% of their annual budget in 8 months. If I catch that, I call them now with a upsell/expansion conversation. Today nobody catches it until it is too late. With OWIsMind alerting me when an account crosses 80% of budget pacing, I am the first call they get. That is a deal.

OUTPUT needed: a push alert or a daily digest I can scan - "these 3 accounts are pacing ahead, call them." Simple, actionable, no noise.

## What Makes Me Trust It Enough to Send to a Client

Three things, non-negotiable:

First - **the number must match what I would find if I looked it up myself.** One wrong number sent to a CFO and I am done. I need to see the source: "this came from your revenue data, period Jan-Dec 2025, actuals only." Not a vague badge - the exact filter that produced the result.

Second - **it must be honest when it does not know.** If I ask about a contract metric it cannot access, I need it to say "I don't have that data" not give me a made-up number with confidence. A tool that says "I don't know" is trustworthy. One that guesses is dangerous.

Third - **I must preview before I send.** A draft email is useful only if I see it before it goes anywhere. No auto-send, ever. I click send, not the system.

---

## 3 Features That Make Me Open It Every Day

1. **Morning portfolio pulse** - I open it, I see which accounts need attention today, ranked. Not a dashboard full of filters - just the answer to "who should I call this week and why."

2. **Export to PNG / copy to clipboard** for every chart and table - frictionless drop into my existing tools (PowerPoint, email, Teams). If I have to reformat anything, I stop using it.

3. **Email draft with one click** - answer a question, click "draft client email," review, send. The whole loop in one place.

---

## What Makes Me Abandon It

One hallucinated number that I sent to a client and had to retract. That is it. One incident. The credibility damage takes months to repair - with the client AND internally. I would rather have a tool that is slower and always right than a fast tool I cannot fully trust.

The second thing: if it takes more than 4 clicks to get to an answer I can use. I am not technical. If the interface requires me to think about how to use it, I use something else.
